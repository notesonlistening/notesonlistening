About

Notes on Listening is a space to explore what music actually does to us.

The joy, the obsession, the communities it creates — and the science behind all of it.

I've always felt music differently. I just never knew why.

There's a specific kind of joy that happens when your favourite artist drops a new album. A specific kind of loss when a song reminds you of someone. A specific kind of belonging when you're in a crowd of strangers who all know every word.

I wanted to understand those feelings — not just experience them. So I started digging. Into the neuroscience, the psychology, the culture. And Notes on Listening was born.

This is not a music review site. There are no star ratings here, no "best albums of the year" lists. Notes on Listening is something different — a publication that treats music as what it actually is: one of the most emotionally complex experiences humans share.

Articles go long. Questions go deep. And personal stories sit alongside peer-reviewed research, because that's how music actually works — it lives in both places at once.

Notes on Listening publishes quarterly, in issues. Each issue has a theme. Everything in it — articles, Deep Cuts, columns, playlists — explores that theme from a different angle.

Issue 00 is The First Time. The first listen. The first artist who made you feel understood. The first time you realised music doesn't hit everyone the same way it hits you.
